BY THE NUMBERS

üìä 0.25% - The Island's inflation rate was the lowest in Canada in June, as reported by Statistics Canada. In June, our inflation rate stood at 0.25%, well below the national average of 2.81%. While Islanders enjoyed a substantial 25% year-over-year drop in the price of gasoline, the 8% increase in grocery costs dampened any potential celebrations. Homeowners are grappling with the impact of higher mortgage interest rates, resulting in a 5.35% increase in the cost of owning a home. Likewise, rental costs have seen a 2.6% increase since June of last year.

üõª 21% - New vehicle sales have shown signs of recovery following a sluggish beginning to the year. This past May, sales increased 21% compared to May 2022, with 707 units sold compared to 583. From January to May, a total of 2,778 vehicles were sold, a slight rise of 3.6% compared to the same period last year, which saw 2,681 vehicles sold. Larger vehicles continue to dominate the market; among the vehicles sold in May, passenger cars accounted for only 14% of the total.
