HISTORY

By Muscle, Mast, and Motor: A Transportation History of Charlottetown

Imagine catching a ferry to Stratford, or hopping on a train to Souris. How about walking on wooden sidewalks, or using a horse and carriage for more than a tourism experience. These are just some methods of transportation covered in a new interactive story map detailing Charlottetown’s transportation history.

Josh MacFadyen, Canada Research Chair in Geospatial Humanities at UPEI, has launched By Muscle, Mast, and Motor: A Transportation History of Charlottetown. The project was created over the last year by MacFadyen and his student Barbara Rousseau, along with other students in the UPEI GeoREACH Lab.

Rich with historical information, photographs, maps, and illustrations, the story map explores the five main ways that people used to travel and transport goods: water, rail, foot, bicycle, and automobile. The project covers the period between the city's beginnings until the interwar period.

HEALTH

NEW MENTAL HEALTH AND ADDICTIONS SERVICES

A new unit is under construction at the Queen Elizabeth Hospital. Located behind the emergency department, the new unit will serve Islanders experiencing mental health and addictions issues.

The Mental Health and Addictions Emergency and Short Stay Unit will provide emergency assessments, treatment, short-stay stabilization, support to transition to other community services, and access to an acute bed when needed.

IGNITION FUND GRANTS

The province has announced the spring 2022 recipients of the Ignition Fund grant. The grant, worth $25,000, is awarded to Islanders seeking start-up capital for a new business venture or to develop and launch a new product. The Ignition Fund has awarded a grant to 75 businesses since it launched in 2014.

CollaborationSchool.Com: A unique training product that helps teams to manage and prevent workplace conflict to decrease stress, increase productivity, and create healthy workplaces. (Kirstin Lund)

L’nu Energy Inc.: Focused on helping First Nation peoples design, construct, and implement cleaner energy systems. (Dylan MacLennan)

Performers’ Health: A health and wellness company that focuses on delivering tailored, accessible, and engaging workouts, nutrition plans, and creative guidance to professional musicians over a digital platform. (Rich Knox)

Sonola Sweets: An Afro-fusion baked goods company that produces bread mixes for customers to create soft and buttery loaves and buns at home. (Rachael Sonola)

Tunistrong Technologies Incorporated : The first and only company to commercialize CNC from tunicates as an ingredient useful for the manufacture of a wide variety of composite materials. (Ian Lucas and Dr. Matthew Dunlop)
