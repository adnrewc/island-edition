Anne of Green Gables trademark dispute

Anne of Green Gables is heading to court in Manhattan. Sort of.


A New York-based theatrical production company has filed a lawsuit in a Manhattan federal court to settle a trademark dispute over the naming rights to Anne of Green Gables.


Producers of "Anne of Green Gables: A New Musical" are fighting allegations that the production infringes on trademarks belonging to Anne of Green Gables Licensing Authority Inc., a company jointly owned by the province of PEI and the heirs of L.M. Montgomery. The authority claims that it is the sole licensing agent for Anne trademarks.


The big city producers have accused Anne of Green Gables Licensing Authority of trying to extend its expired copyright in the book through trademark threats. The lawsuit claims that the licensing authority issued a draft version of a trademark infringement complaint and threatened to file it in court if the producers did not rename their musical, which is being developed for Broadway.


This story broke Tuesday evening when news of the lawsuit was first reported by Reuters.
