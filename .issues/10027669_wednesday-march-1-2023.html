Confed Centre's $100M Dream

More than $100M worth of renovations are envisioned for the Confederation Centre of the Arts. The plan is split into two phases; phase one focuses on work to the southern end of the complex along Victoria Row, while work along Grafton Street, which includes extending the complex to the sidewalk, would come later. 


Construction of a rehearsal hall over what is now an outdoor plaza is a major component of the project, as well as rebuilding entrances and reconfiguring space inside the centre. Rooftop solar would be installed, an effort that would reduce the centre‚Äôs emissions by nearly 50 per cent.


Phase one is expected to cost $71.5M. According to a plan presented to Charlottetown council Monday night, staff hope the federal government will chip in $30M toward the project, $20M from the province, and $5M from the city. The centre would fundraise the remaining $16.5M. Current estimates for phase two land around $30M, but funding efforts for that portion of the project haven't begun.

If funding is secured, construction of phase one would would wrap up in 2026.
